# 05 — Audience profile

> Two named personas. An ICP definition with hard qualifying criteria. Anti-personas with hard disqualifiers. The intake call uses this doc as a literal checklist.

## ICP — qualifying criteria

A retainer is a fit when **all five** of the following are true:

1. **Company stage** — B2B SaaS / services / fintech / dev-tools, post-product-market-fit, $2M-$60M ARR, or growth-stage funded ($2M+ raised in last 36 months).
2. **Audience size** — the principal already has between 800 and 8,000 LinkedIn followers. Below 800 we cannot accelerate fast enough; above 8,000 the principal's voice is already public and our value-add narrows to comment + DM work.
3. **Time-deficit, not skill-deficit** — the principal has previously published 5+ LinkedIn posts that we judge as "real" (a real story, real opinion, written by them) but has stopped posting in the last 90 days due to time constraints. We are not a writing teacher.
4. **Decision-maker** — the principal is the budget owner (founder, C-suite, or VP-level). Marketing-led intros where "the CEO will eventually approve" are a no.
5. **Voice-defendable** — on the intake call, the principal can articulate **two POVs they would defend in a board meeting**. If they can articulate "thought leadership about leadership" but no actual contested opinion, we send them back.

**The job-to-be-done.** "I have a CEO who will eventually want me to be visible. I have a network that respects me. I have stories. I do not have the six hours/week to write."

**Where they buy** — sees a ghostwritten post from a peer; clicks profile; finds the agency; books a call. Or — referred by an existing Underline retainer in their professional circle.

**Objections that close the sale** — pricing transparency on the page (no "book a call to learn pricing"); the anti-feature manifesto in the contract; the explicit one-month exit clause.

**Objections that lose the sale** — "we cap at six retainers per cohort and we're full" — the wait-list converts ~25% in two months but loses ~75%.

## Anti-personas (hard disqualifiers)

We say no on the intake call when **any** of:

- **Solopreneur / coach / creator** with no team behind them. We're built for principals inside a company.
- **Pre-PMF startup** ($0-500k ARR) — wrong stage; ROI math doesn't work; voice is still mutating.
- **Influencer-economy / personal-brand crowd** — they want hooks, pods, and follower-count optimisation; we don't sell those.
- **D2C / consumer brand operator** — wrong audience; LinkedIn isn't their channel.
- **"Thought leadership" with no thought** — on the intake call, the principal cannot name a single contested POV. We send the deposit back.
- **Compliance-impossible verticals** — primary-care medical advice, securities recommendations, legal-defence content. We tell them we can write *around* the topic but not *about* the topic.
- **Existing engagement-pod participant** — if the principal already runs in pods and refuses to leave them on retainer, we decline. We can't underwrite the brand-decay risk.
- **"Make me viral" expectation** — virality and reach are not our promise. If a customer judges us on virality, we will fail them.

## Channels they live in (audience media diet)

LinkedIn (reading, not writing) → Stratechery / Lex / Lenny's email → *Acquired*, *In Depth*, *Invest Like the Best* podcasts → Pavilion / GTM Partners / Reforge alumni Slacks → quiet group chats with peers.

The implication: our owned channel cannot be *more* LinkedIn-y than the audience already lives in. It has to feel like a Stratechery email, not like a LinkedIn carousel.
