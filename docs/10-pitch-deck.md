## Slide 2 — Problem

The B2B founders and senior operators who **should** be writing publicly on LinkedIn — VPs, COOs, second-time founders, CROs — are not.

The reason isn't taste, isn't intent, isn't audience. It's time.

- A real LinkedIn post takes 90 minutes to draft well.
- A real comment plan takes 2-3 hours a week.
- A real DM book takes 30 minutes a day.

That's six hours a week. Maya, our customer, has 1.5.

The market response is wrong. LinkedIn coaches teach the founder to write (six more hours). Ghostwriters write in their own voice, not the founder's. Engagement-pod SaaS damages the brand it claims to build.

There is a gap. The gap is *a literary agency for the channel*.

---

## Slide 3 — Solution

A monthly retainer that ships:

1. Three ghostwritten posts per week, in the founder's voice (not ours).
2. An editorial comment plan against fifty target accounts (15 hand-written comments/week).
3. A living DM book — drafts the founder presses send on.
4. A weekly editorial call.
5. CRM webhook routing — DMs that show intent become deals in HubSpot or Pipedrive.
6. A monthly accuracy & health report.

We are six writers. We cap intake at six retainers per cohort. The cap is the brand.

---

## Slide 4 — Why now

- LinkedIn organic reach for B2B has *recovered* in the last 18 months for accounts with strong original POV, while collapsing for everyone else. The middle ground (lazy posts, hook bait) is dead. The ceiling for thoughtful operators is higher than it has been in five years.
- The "personal brand" SaaS market peaked in 2024 at ~$420M ARR and has declined ~22% since as detection systems caught up with engagement-pod patterns.
- Stablecoin checkout (NOWPayments, Plisio) finally lets us collect international retainer revenue without 3-5% card-processing tax — material at $1,490-$4,990/mo retainers across 12 time zones.
- AI-drafting tools collapsed the cost of getting from outline to first draft, but failed to capture voice. Voice is now the moat. Bylineship is built around voice capture, not draft speed.

## Slide 5 — Product

**The retainer is a calendar, not a tool.**

Monday — 60-min editorial call. Three story angles pulled from the founder's week.
Tuesday — Post #1 ships at 7 am. Comment-engine queue lands at 4 pm.
Wednesday — Senior writer works the comment plan. 15 comments by hand, signed off in the founder's name.
Thursday — Post #2 ships. DM book updated.
Friday — Post #3 ships. Founder presses send on the four DMs the writers drafted. Replies-with-intent flow into the CRM via webhook.

The deliverable is the cadence, not a piece of software.

- ~18,000 B2B SaaS companies in this range globally (Crunchbase + Apollo + ZoomInfo cross-reference, 2025).
- ~2-3 retainable principals per company (CEO + COO + VP RevOps or VP Customer Success).
- Addressable principals: ~45,000 globally.
- Realistically reachable cohort (within our channels — referral, organic, peer Slacks): ~3,500.

Adjacent expansion: agency principals (CROs running 4-8 voices), public-company executive comms teams, a single-vertical specialty agency in fintech (we may spin one out).
