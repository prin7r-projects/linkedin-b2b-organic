Problem

Maya has 1.5 hours a week. Posting takes six.

The B2B founders and senior operators who should be writing publicly on LinkedIn — VPs, COOs, second-time founders, CROs — are not. The reason isn’t taste, isn’t intent, isn’t audience. It’s time.

  • A real LinkedIn post takes 90 minutes to draft well.
  • A real comment plan takes 2–3 hours a week.
  • A real DM book takes 30 minutes a day.

LinkedIn coaches teach the founder to write (six more hours). Ghostwriters write in their own voice, not the founder’s. Engagement-pod SaaS damages the brand it claims to build. There is a gap.

The gap is a literary agency for the channel02 / 10
03 / 10

Solution

A retainer that ships, weekly.

  • Three ghostwritten posts per week, in the founder’s voice (not ours).
  • An editorial comment plan against fifty target accounts — 15 hand-written comments per week.
  • A living DM book — drafts the founder presses send on.
  • A weekly editorial call.
  • CRM webhook routing — DMs that show intent become deals in HubSpot or Pipedrive.
  • A monthly accuracy & health report.

We are six writers. We cap intake at six retainers per cohort. The cap is the brand.

The deliverable is the cadence03 / 10

Why now

Voice is the moat. AI made it cheaper to capture.

  • LinkedIn organic reach for B2B has recovered for accounts with strong original POV; collapsed for everyone else. The middle ground is dead.
  • The "personal brand" SaaS market peaked in 2024 at ~$420M ARR and has declined ~22% since as detection systems caught up with engagement-pod patterns.
  • Stablecoin checkout (NOWPayments, Plisio) finally lets us collect international retainer revenue without 3-5% card-processing tax — material at $1,490-$4,990/mo across 12 time zones.
  • AI-drafting tools collapsed the cost of getting from outline to first draft. Voice is now the moat. Underline is built around voice capture, not draft speed.
