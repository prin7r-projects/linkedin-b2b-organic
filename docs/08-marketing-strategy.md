# 08 — Marketing strategy

> Underline's marketing is its work. We don't broadcast; we execute and the execution is the marketing. This doc captures positioning, messaging hierarchy, content pillars, and the editorial principles a writer or designer should hold in front of them when they touch any Underline-owned surface.

## Positioning

**Category.** A literary agency for B2B LinkedIn.

The category is *new*. There are LinkedIn coaches, ghostwriters, content agencies, and personal-brand SaaS — but no one positions as a literary agency. We chose this category for three reasons:

1. **It tells the audience how we think about the writing** — slowly, by hand, with refusal as a feature.
2. **It signals to anti-personas** — the literary-agency frame is wrong for influencer-economy, follower-count, hook-bait, and engagement-pod customers. They self-deselect.
3. **It puts a ceiling on us** — a literary agency cap is six clients per cohort. The frame keeps us honest about scale.

## Positioning statement

For B2B founders and senior operators who know LinkedIn is the right channel but cannot find the six hours a week to write well, Underline is a literary agency that ships three ghostwritten posts a week in your voice, an editorial comment plan against fifty target accounts, and a CRM-routed DM book — unlike LinkedIn coaches (who teach you to write but never write) and unlike growth hackers (who run engagement pods and bot comments) because we are six writers, we cap intake at six retainers per cohort, and we keep a printed list of things we will not do that we send back signed with every contract.

## Messaging hierarchy

### Layer 1 — One sentence (the headline)

> You should be writing. We'll do it.

Used in the hero. Five words plus a contraction. Rejects every common LinkedIn-agency hook ("scale your personal brand", "build your audience", "10x your reach").

### Layer 2 — One paragraph (the sub-hero / cold-intro reply)

> Underline is a literary agency for B2B LinkedIn. Three ghostwritten posts a week, in your voice. An editorial comment plan on fifty target accounts. A DM book that lands meetings. No engagement pods, no bots, no clickbait hooks. Six writers, six retainers per cohort.

### Layer 3 — One page (the landing)

The full landing at `linkedin-b2b-organic.prin7r.com`. Eight sections in this order:
1. Masthead
2. Hero (headline + sample ghostwritten post)
3. Service ledger (six numbered items)
4. The week (Mon-Fri cadence)
5. Anti-feature manifesto ("Six things we won't do")
6. Pricing (three retainers + agency callout)
7. FAQ (seven questions)
8. Footer

### Layer 4 — One conversation (the intake call)

30 minutes, head writer, no slides, no demo. Covers the five qualifying criteria from `docs/05-audience-profile.md` (stage, audience size, time-deficit, decision-maker, voice-defendable) and ends with the two-POV check.

## Content pillars (the three things we write about)

Every Underline marketing artefact (and every comment on the comment-engine when it surfaces our voice) ladders up to one of three pillars. We do not write outside them.

### Pillar 1 — Craft

The actual mechanics of writing well on LinkedIn. Sentence-level, structural, editorial. Examples we'd publish:

- *"What we changed when our average comment dwell time went from 2 to 9 minutes."*
- *"The seven hooks we refuse, and what we use instead."*
- *"Why we draft Tuesday-for-Tuesday, not Sunday-for-Tuesday."*

### Pillar 2 — Refusal

What we don't do, and why. The list grows; we don't shorten it. Examples:

- The anti-manifesto on the landing.
- *"We sent the deposit back twice last cohort. Here's why."*
- *"Why our comment-engine writes 15/wk, not 100/wk."*

### Pillar 3 — Slow truth

What the data actually says about LinkedIn organic for B2B — including the unflattering parts (reach is collapsing for most accounts; comment-engine ROI is real but slow; founder-led audience growth is asymptotic at ~12k for most B2B operators). We don't sugarcoat. The audience is mid-career and skeptical; pretending otherwise erodes trust.

Examples:
- *"After 73 retainers, here's what month-13 retention actually looks like."*
- *"The three accounts in our cohort that we judge as net-negative-credibility on LinkedIn."*
- *"We changed our comment-engine after a target-account writer told us our comment was 'cringe.' Here's the new method."*

## Tone — three do's, three don'ts

**Do.**
1. Open with action — first sentence drops the reader into a moment.
2. Name a single concrete artefact — a number, a calendar, a meeting.
3. Be willing to be a little embarrassed.

**Don't.**
1. Hook bait. ("Most VPs fail at this — here's why.") No.
2. Manufactured controversy. ("Unpopular opinion:" pretending to be unpopular.) No.
3. Emoji in product copy.

## Channels we own

|---|---|---|
| Landing page | `linkedin-b2b-organic.prin7r.com` | Every retainer-shape change; quarterly review |

| Telegram retainer channels | Per-customer, private | Daily during retainer |
| Pavilion / GTM Partners / Reforge / Acquired Slacks | Public read-only participation | Twice/week; never broadcast |

## Channels we deliberately don't own

- A blog.
- A newsletter.
- A YouTube channel.
- A Twitter/X account beyond the head writer's personal handle.
- A podcast.

We are a writers' room, not a media company. Adding any of those would split focus and degrade the editorial standard.

## Marketing assets we will and won't ship

**Will.**
- The landing.
- A one-page handover doc when a retainer ends ("Voice profile, all transcripts, your editorial calendar, your comment list, your DM book — yours forever.").
- A quarterly internal write-up of one experiment, posted into one of our owned Slack channels.

**Won't.**
- Case-study PDFs with logos.
- Capability decks.
- A "ROI calculator."
- A "free LinkedIn audit" lead magnet.
- A "writing course" upsell.

## Brand pyramid → execution

| Layer | Decision |
|---|---|
| Essence (Editorial) | Every artefact is reviewed by a writer before it ships. |
| Personality (Patient) | We say "we'll wait-list you" instead of "claim your spot." |

| Personality (Craft-proud) | We talk about comment dwell time in marketing. |
| Value (Taste) | The hero shows a real-looking ghostwritten post, not a logo grid. |
| Value (Refusal) | "Six things we won't do" is in the contract verbatim. |

## Annual marketing review (held in January)

- One question, holding everyone accountable: *"Did anything we shipped this year embarrass us?"*
- If yes: we kill it.
- If no: we ship more of it next year.
